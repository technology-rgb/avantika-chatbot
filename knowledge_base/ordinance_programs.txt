=== ORDINANCE NO. 26: BACHELOR OF TECHNOLOGY (B.Tech) ===

Program Overview:
- Duration: 4 years / 8 semesters
- Full-time degree program in Engineering & Technology
- Under NEP 2020, AICTE and State Government of Madhya Pradesh guidelines
- Medium of instruction: English (other languages may be used as per regulatory requirements)

Admission Eligibility:
- Minimum 45% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in 10+2 (or equivalent)
- As per AICTE / State Government of Madhya Pradesh / UGC guidelines
- The minimum qualification is decided by the respective Board of Studies and approved by Academic Council of the University

Examination Structure:
- First Year: First Semester Exam + Second Semester Exam
- Second Year: Third Semester Exam + Fourth Semester Exam
- Third Year: Fifth Semester Exam + Sixth Semester Exam
- Fourth Year: Seventh Semester Exam + Eighth Semester Exam

Assessment Components:
- CCE (Continuous Comprehensive Evaluation): assignments, quizzes, practicals, lab work, class participation
- End Semester Examination: time-bound written exams
- Practical / Lab examinations
- Major Project (final year)
- Compulsory Internship/Industrial Training (third or fourth year)

Internship:
- Internship is compulsory as part of the B.Tech program
- The Major Project pertains to the internship, jointly assessed by the University and the host organization
- Decision on equivalent courses for internship taken by the respective Board of Studies approved by Academic Council

ATKT (Allowed to Keep Terms) Rules:
- A student is allowed to appear in next year/semester examinations even with failed subjects (backlogs), subject to prescribed limits
- Maximum number of backlog subjects to continue to the next year/semester is specified in Academic Regulations
- A student cannot proceed to the next higher year if backlog count exceeds the prescribed limit
- Students must clear all backlogs within the maximum program duration

Maximum Program Duration:
- B.Tech program must be completed within 8 years (double the 4-year normal duration)
- Students who cannot complete within this duration may be considered for re-admission as per university policy

GRADING SYSTEM (Choice Based Credit System — applicable to all programs):

Grade | % Marks Range | Grade Points | Description
A+    | 90 – 100      | 10           | Outstanding
A     | 81 – 90       | 9            | Excellent
B+    | 71 – 80       | 8            | Very Good
B     | 61 – 70       | 7            | Good
C     | 51 – 60       | 6            | Average
D     | 41 – 50       | 5            | Satisfactory
F     | Below 41      | 0            | Fail
FR    | —             | 0            | Fail due to Ragging
W     | —             | 0            | Withdrawn

Note: FR grade is awarded when a student is found guilty of ragging. W grade is awarded on withdrawal from a course.

SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average):
SGPA = Σ(Ci × Gi) / ΣCi
where Ci = credit of i-th subject in that semester, Gi = grade point obtained in i-th subject

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average):
CGPA = Σ(Ci × Si) / ΣCi
where Si = SGPA of i-th semester, Ci = total credits in that semester

Percentage Conversion:
Percentage = CGPA × 10

CGPA to Division (Award of Degree — B.Tech):
CGPA Score           | Division
8.5 and above        | First Class with Distinction
6.5 to below 8.5     | First Class
5.5 to below 6.5     | Second Class
4.5 to below 5.5     | Pass Class
Below 4.5            | Fail

Award of B.Tech Degree:
- Must pass all 8 semesters and all prescribed subjects/courses
- Must complete compulsory internship
- Must fulfil credit requirements as prescribed by Board of Studies
- No outstanding dues to the University
- No pending disciplinary proceedings

Promotion to Higher Year (B.Tech):
- First to Second Year: may be promoted with backlogs not exceeding prescribed limit
- Second to Third Year: may be promoted with backlogs not exceeding prescribed limit
- Third to Fourth Year: may be promoted with backlogs not exceeding prescribed limit
- Academic probation applies to students who exceed backlog limits

Re-admission:
- Candidates unable to complete program within stipulated time may be considered for re-admission as per university policy
- Subject to Academic Council approval

=== ORDINANCE NO. 27: BACHELOR OF DESIGN (B.Des) ===

Program Overview:
- Duration: 4 years / 8 semesters
- Full-time degree program in Design
- Under NEP 2020 and relevant regulatory body (AICTE/UGC)
- Studio-based and project-based pedagogy

Admission Eligibility:
- Minimum qualification as per provisions of the relevant Regulatory Body, AICTE, UGC and University guidelines
- Portfolio/aptitude test may be required as part of admissions process
- As per State Government of Madhya Pradesh guidelines

Examination Structure:
- Same semester-wise structure as B.Tech (8 semesters over 4 years)
- Jury-based assessment panels for design studio work (internal + external experts)
- Rubric-based evaluation using standardised assessment sheets
- Theory subjects may have written examinations
- Major Design Project in final year

Assessment:
- CCE through studio reviews, critiques, assignments, participation
- Jury Assessment: formal end-of-semester panels evaluate projects
- Venue-wise lists displayed at least 3 days before Jury

Grading, SGPA, CGPA: Same grading system as B.Tech (A+ to F/FR/W)

CGPA to Division (B.Des):
Same criteria as B.Tech (8.5+ Distinction, 6.5+ First Class, 5.5+ Second Class, 4.5+ Pass)

ATKT Rules, Maximum Duration, Award of Degree: Same framework as B.Tech

=== GENERAL PROVISIONS (ALL PROGRAMS) ===

Credit Mapping and Transfer:
- Subjects/courses carry credits as prescribed by Board of Studies and approved by Academic Council
- MOOC/online course credit transfer applicable as per NEP 2020 guidelines (specific credits as prescribed)
- Add-on courses may contribute to credit count as per regulations

Academic Integrity:
- UFM (Unfair Means) provisions apply to all programs
- Ragging results in FR grade (Fail due to Ragging) for that semester
- Academic misconduct handled by Disciplinary/UFM Committee

Re-examination Policy:
- Supplementary/Backlog examinations available for failed subjects
- Makeup examinations for students with approved absence
- Improvement examinations to improve grades (original CCE carried forward)
- Application process: School Dean signs, fees paid, application submitted to Exam Office

Attendance Requirements:
- Minimum attendance as per University regulations required to appear in examinations
- Students below minimum attendance threshold may be detained from appearing in exams
- Medical/special leave may be considered with proper documentation

Grading Remarks:
- Incomplete (I): awarded when CCE/work is not completed; student must complete within next semester
- W (Withdrawn): student withdraws from course before completion; zero grade points
- FR (Fail due to Ragging): awarded on proven ragging; zero grade points; appears on transcript

Transcript:
- Official transcripts issued by Controller of Examinations (CoE)
- Grades for each course, SGPA for each semester, and CGPA listed
- Percentage equivalent (CGPA × 10) stated as per CoE notification
- Transcript requested through Student Office with No-Dues clearance

Award of Degree — Common Conditions:
1. Passed all prescribed courses/subjects in all semesters
2. Completed compulsory internship/project as required by program
3. Fulfilled minimum credit requirements as prescribed
4. No outstanding fee dues to the University
5. No pending disciplinary proceedings
6. Fulfilled attendance requirements

Division is awarded only after completion of the full program, based on overall CGPA.

Governing Authority:
- Academic Council of Avantika University is the supreme academic body
- Board of Studies (per School/Department) prescribes curriculum, credits, evaluation schemes
- Controller of Examinations (CoE) administers examinations and results
- Vice Chancellor approves final decisions on degree awards, special cases
- UGC/AICTE regulations and NEP 2020 framework govern overall academic structure
